# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three audiences, all Arabic-speaking, mostly Saudi:
- Clients seeking a contractor (government or private): evaluate credibility before requesting a quote.
- Job seekers: check the training/employment page (تدريب وتوظيف) for openings and programs.
- Partners and suppliers: check legitimacy and scale before doing business.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for لمعة العربية للمقاولات (Lamat Elarabia Contracting), a Riyadh-based contracting company. Site exists to build trust with prospective clients and partners, surface news/press, and support hiring. Success looks like: visitors trust the company's scale and legitimacy within seconds, and job seekers or clients know exactly how to make contact (WhatsApp, email, phone all present in footer).

## Brand Personality

Solid, national, trustworthy. Heavy, dependable, patriotic tone. Reflected in the existing tagline: "نبني اليوم… لنصنع مستقبلًا يليق بوطننا" (we build today to make a future worthy of our nation). Not modern-sleek-startup; not flashy. A company that builds physical, permanent things.

## Anti-references

Must not read as an unedited WordPress/Elementor/Astra template: default widget spacing, stock icon packs, generic clip-art imagery, template-shaped card grids. Also avoid flashy tech-startup visual language (gradients, glassmorphism, neon accents) - wrong register for a contracting company's credibility signal.

## Design Principles

- Credibility over cleverness: every polish decision should read as "established, serious company," not "cool app."
- RTL is load-bearing, not an afterthought: Arabic reading order and right-alignment must be correct everywhere, not patched with hacks.
- Contact must never be more than one scroll away: phone/WhatsApp/email/address are the conversion point for all three audiences.
- Preserve the existing visual identity (palette, tagline, logo) while removing template seams - this is a polish pass, not a redesign.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- RTL correctness across all pages and components (known trouble spot: current CSS has manual RTL overrides for icon lists and menus).
- WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive elements.
- General Saudi public including older, non-technical users: generous text sizes and tap targets, no reliance on hover-only affordances.
